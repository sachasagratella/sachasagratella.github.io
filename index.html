Academic · Insurance

Extreme Value Theory on US Health Insurance Claims

Applied Extreme Value Theory (EVT) — GEV and GPD distributions — to a large US health insurance claims dataset to model the tail behaviour of catastrophic claim events. Estimated Value-at-Risk (VaR) and Expected Shortfall (ES) at extreme quantiles.

Results inform reserve adequacy and reinsurance strategy for tail-risk events, directly relevant to catastrophe pricing in the insurance sector.

R EVT / GEV / GPD VaR Expected Shortfall Insurance Risk
